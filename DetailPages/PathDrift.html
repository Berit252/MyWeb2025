Path Drift

To function properly as social beings, we begin learning rules from the moment we’re born. The colour of a signal light, the meaning of a warning tone, the etiquette of clothing — all these conventions seep into us so naturally that we sometimes wonder whether our responses are conditioned or instinctive. This domestication is so seamless that it rarely triggers resistance. Whether social rules truly reshape our genes is impossible to prove, but it's clear that these countless 'rules' have already created heterotopic spaces.


为了做好一个社会动物，人被生下来就要学习各种规矩。像是指示灯的颜色、提示音的含义、服饰的礼仪等。我们甚至会怀疑这些习得的行为属于条件反射还是非条件反射。因为这些驯化十分自然，它不会让人有太多逆反情绪。人类社会的规矩是否真的在潜移默化地影响人类基因，这难以考证。但可以得知的是因为种种的“规矩”，异托邦场域的已形成。人类会自主地进入这些规则迷宫，习得和探索出逃的办法，然后再进入下一个迷宫。



Life seems to be such a cycle: being constrained -> breaking free from constraints -> building or finding the next constraint -> then trying to escape the new rules. It's like playing puzzle and maze games, where players find enjoyment in struggling and seeking freedom.


而人生似乎就是这样的循环：受到束缚 -> 挣脱束缚 -> 构建或找到下一个约束 -> 再尝试跳出新的规则约束。就像玩各种解密和迷宫游戏，玩家在抗争和探寻自由的过程中找到乐趣。



Drawing on theories from Foucault’s 'Discipline and Punish' and my own experiences, I designed this game as a way for players to navigate and escape a series of mazes shaped by the rules they have absorbed in both life and gameplay. Through this process, I hope to open up a space for thinking about multiplicity of perspective, discipline, and the meaning of freedom.


我结合了福柯在《规训与惩罚》中提出的部分概念和我的个人经验，设计这个游戏，试图让玩家通过在生活和游戏中习得的规则，逃离一个又一个迷宫。并希望在游戏过程中探讨视角多样性、规训和自由的议题。

TECHNICAL TOOLS: Blender, UE5, SD, PR
